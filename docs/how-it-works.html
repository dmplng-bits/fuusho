How It Works

fuusho — a sealed letter, not a postcard

Two ways to read this page: the plain version if you just want to understand what happens when you use it, and the architecture below it if you're the one deploying or integrating with it.

In Plain Terms

What actually happens

No accounts, no third-party company in the middle. Just a box you own and a phone you own, introduced to each other once.

01

Run fuusho on something you own

A spare computer, a Raspberry Pi, a cheap VPS — anywhere you can run a container. It's the one place your notification content ever passes through.

02

Scan a QR code once

Your server shows a code. Your phone scans it. That's the entire setup — like pairing a Bluetooth speaker, not filling out a form.

03

Every notification is sealed

From then on, whatever your server sends is locked before it leaves — only your phone holds the key that opens it.

Pairing handshake

What happens between a QR scan and a device showing up in GET /api/devices — the part that replaces a shared secret with an X25519 key exchange.

ECDH + HKDF → transport key

Only public keys and ciphertext ever cross the network The push-encryption key is generated on the phone, never sent by the server
